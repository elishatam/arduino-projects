Escape The Room: Weight Puzzle
OVERVIEW | HARDWARE | SOFTWARE





MECHANICAL STRUCTURE
The puzzle is made of up 5 scales, and each scale contains a load cell. The strain gauge load cell that measures electrical resistance changes in response to deformation caused by a force. This load cell can read up to 10kg (22lbs).

The load cell is mounted in a "Z-formation" between 2 plastic (ABS plastic) pieces. There are 4 strain gauges on the load cell. When torque is applied to the bar and bends the bar, the strain gauges will measure the bending distortion: 2 measure compression and 2 measure tension. When these 4 strain gauges are set up in a wheatstone bridge formation, the load cell accurately measures small changes in resistance and outputs a resulting voltage.

From there the voltage is passed to an amplifier for a more measurable signal.





ELECTRICAL BLOCK DIAGRAM
Each of the 5 scales is connected to an amplifier circuit and the output is connected to a GPIO pin on the Arduino microcontroller.

A magnetic lock is connected to a relay which is controlled by the Arduino. Based on the weights the Arduino reads from the scales, the Arduino will open or close the relay to unlock or lock the magnetic lock.

A push button and LED is used for user interface.
